About LEGASI

Inspired by a vision of a peaceful and empowered community free from violence and hate

Our Journey Towards Empowerment

LEGASI (Ladies Empowerment Goals and Support Initiative) was founded in 2010 with a mission to empower women and promote peace in conflict-affected communities across Nigeria. Our organization emerged as a response to the increasing violence and discrimination faced by women in northern Nigeria.

Over the past decade, we've grown from a small community initiative to a nationally recognized organization with programs in 12 states. Our approach combines grassroots engagement with evidence-based strategies to address the root causes of conflict and inequality.

Read More About Us

Our Vision

"A peaceful and empowered community free from violence and hate"

We envision Nigerian communities where diversity is celebrated, conflicts are resolved through dialogue, and all members have equal opportunities to thrive regardless of gender, ethnicity, or religion.

Our Mission

"To promote peaceful co-existence in communities through effective community dialogue and mediation, evidence-based advocacy, political and socio-economic empowerment"

We work directly with communities to build sustainable peace through locally-driven solutions and empowerment programs that address both immediate needs and long-term systemic change.

Our Objectives

Raise awareness on education, reproductive health, and poverty alleviation for young women and girls, providing age-appropriate sex education and prevention strategies for teen pregnancies and underage marriages.

Offer skill acquisition, ICT training, mentoring, and support networks to help girls and women achieve financial independence.

Core Values

Integrity: We maintain the highest ethical standards in all our operations.

Accountability: We take responsibility for our actions and their impact.

Transparency: We operate openly and share information about our work.

Inclusivity: We embrace diversity and ensure all voices are heard.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our work and how you can get involved

How does LEGASI select communities to work with?

We prioritize communities based on several factors: level of conflict intensity, presence of vulnerable populations (especially women and children), community willingness to engage in peacebuilding, and potential for sustainable impact. Our selection process involves extensive research, community consultations, and partnership with local authorities.

What types of vocational training do you provide?

Our vocational training programs are tailored to local economic opportunities and include: tailoring and fashion design, agriculture and agro-processing, soap and cosmetic production, ICT skills, small business management, and craft-making. Each program includes business development training to help participants establish sustainable income sources.

How can I volunteer with LEGASI?

We welcome volunteers with diverse skills. Opportunities include: field program support, capacity building trainers, administrative assistance, fundraising specialists, and technical experts. Please visit our Volunteer page to see current openings and application requirements. All volunteers undergo background checks and orientation training.

How transparent is LEGASI with donations?

Transparency is a core value. We publish annual reports with detailed financial statements, program outcomes, and impact assessments. Donors receive regular updates on how their contributions are used. We're also certified by the Nigerian Financial Intelligence Unit (SCUML) and maintain the highest standards of financial accountability.

How do you measure the impact of your programs?

We use a comprehensive monitoring and evaluation framework that tracks both quantitative and qualitative indicators. These include: number of conflicts resolved, economic indicators for participants, pre/post assessments of trauma symptoms, community perception surveys, and longitudinal studies on peace sustainability. All reports are publicly available on our website.
